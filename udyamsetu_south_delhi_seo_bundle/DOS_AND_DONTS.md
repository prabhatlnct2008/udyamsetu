# Dos and Don'ts — South Delhi Local SEO Bundle

## What makes these pages strong

- They target distinct commercial intents rather than ten versions of the same “best agency in South Delhi” page.
- They combine local SEO with conversion logic: landing pages, WhatsApp qualification, lead routing, reporting, and proof.
- They use South Delhi neighborhoods as real business context: Saket clinics, GK premium services, Nehru Place IT, Okhla B2B suppliers, South Extension showrooms, Lajpat Nagar retail/services.
- They avoid generic AI hype by describing workflows, human approval, data readiness, and measurable outcomes.
- They support internal linking across AI, automation, lead generation, SEO, websites, and industry pages.

## How to avoid thin content

- Do not clone one page and swap locality names.
- Each page must have a unique audience, pain point, CTA, FAQ set, proof block, and schema plan.
- Add business-specific examples, not only keyword phrases.
- Use case studies, screenshots, diagrams, sample scripts, or page maps wherever possible.
- Include a local relevance section that explains why the page matters to that kind of South Delhi business.

## How to avoid over-optimization

- Use the primary keyword in the SEO title, H1, introduction, and one or two body sections only where natural.
- Do not repeat “South Delhi” in every sentence.
- Use synonyms: Delhi NCR, local businesses, service-area, Saket, GK, Nehru Place, Okhla, nearby areas.
- Prioritize readability and conversion clarity over exact-match density.
- Use FAQs for natural long-tail coverage.

## How to keep pages locally relevant

- Mention South Delhi localities only when tied to a real buyer situation.
- Add service-area maps only where useful.
- Use examples by business type and micro-market.
- Add local trust blocks: industries served, service-area statement, local page examples, and portfolio cards.
- Keep the page useful for someone who actually runs a business in South Delhi.

## What not to repeat across all 10 pages

Avoid repeating the same paragraph about:

- “We are the best agency in South Delhi.”
- “We help businesses grow online.”
- “AI is transforming every industry.”
- “Contact us for digital marketing services.”
- Identical locality lists.
- Identical CTA copy without matching the page intent.

## Trust/proof/content blocks that should appear

Use different proof blocks depending on page type:

- AI pages: workflow map, pilot scorecard, governance and human approval rules.
- Automation pages: before/after lead flow, WhatsApp script, CRM-lite pipeline screenshot.
- Lead generation pages: lead quality scorecard, source tracking report, page map.
- SEO pages: search footprint map, safe citation inventory, no-spam link policy.
- Website pages: mobile mockups, page architecture, launch QA checklist.
- Clinic page: approved message flow, appointment reminder examples, no-diagnosis policy.
- Real estate page: buyer qualification questions, site-visit pipeline, CPL/quality reporting.
- Nehru Place/Okhla page: RFQ form, product category page, quote follow-up workflow.

## Local SEO writing best practices for this bundle

- Start with the buyer’s operational problem.
- Add local context in the first 150–250 words without forcing it.
- Use one clear H1 and logical H2 sections.
- Include internal links to relevant service and industry pages.
- Add FAQPage schema when FAQs are visible on the page.
- Add Service schema with `areaServed` for South Delhi and selected localities.
- Avoid fake addresses if the business does not publish a physical South Delhi office.
- Use WhatsApp CTAs because UdyamSetu’s existing positioning is WhatsApp-first.
- Track conversion events for WhatsApp clicks, forms, calls, and qualified lead status.

## Page-specific publishing checklist

Before publishing each page, confirm:

- Unique SEO title and meta description.
- Unique H1.
- Primary keyword appears naturally.
- CTA matches the visitor intent.
- FAQs are page-specific.
- Schema type fits the page.
- Internal links point to the most relevant pages.
- The page has a trust/proof block.
- The page has a visual concept, not only text.
- The page avoids claims that cannot be proven.
